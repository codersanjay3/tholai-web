# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Primary**: Women 18–34, skincare-curious, TikTok/Instagram-native, willing to pay for personalised tools. Uses the product at home during morning/night routines.
- **Secondary**: Men 20–40 starting a skincare routine for the first time — need guidance, not judgment.
- **Tertiary**: Skintellectuals — enthusiasts who want ingredient analysis and optimisation.

Primary job: "Tell me exactly what my skin needs and how to fix it, without overwhelming me."

## Product Purpose

AI-powered web skincare app. Analyses the user's face via photo, identifies skin type and concerns, then generates personalised product recommendations and AM/PM routines. Tailored to user-defined goals and monthly budget. Subscription-gated with a free scan. Revenue via subscription tiers and affiliate product links.

Success = user sees measurable skin improvement tracked via weekly photo check-ins (Glow Score), stays subscribed, and trusts the app over generic beauty advice.

## Brand Personality

Soft, clinical, trusted — like a dermatologist friend: warm but science-backed, clean and precise. Confident without being cold.

## Anti-references

- Millennial pink gradients, rose-gold, Sephora-clone aesthetics
- Hospital white, blue-tinted, sterile pharmacy-app look
- Gray-on-gray dark SaaS / enterprise dashboard aesthetic
- Loud fonts, chaotic layout, Gen-Z meme/TikTok energy
- Cream/sand/beige backgrounds that read as generic AI output
- Hero metric templates with big numbers and gradient accents

## Design Principles

1. **Precision earns trust** — every UI element should feel considered, not decorative. If it can't earn its place, remove it.
2. **Warmth without softness** — approachable but not pastel. The brand feel comes from typography and layout, not tinted backgrounds.
3. **Science made legible** — AI output (skin analysis, concern scores, ingredient data) must feel expert but readable, not intimidating.
4. **Progress is the product** — the Glow Score and photo timeline are the core retention mechanic; design should make progress feel visible and motivating.
5. **Deferred friction** — account creation happens after value is demonstrated, not before. Gate last, not first.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text)
- Reduced motion support required on all animations
- Diverse skin tone representation in UI imagery (Fitzpatrick I–VI)
